If you don't already have a solar panel at your house, you should get some. After federal tax incentives, solar panels only cost about $12,000 (for a 6 kW system). The average cost per kilowatt hour (kWh) with solar panels is 6-8 cents. Solar Panel Cost (solar.com) On the other hand, it costs an average of 13.19 cents per kWh to have electricity without solar panels. Electric Rates by State (electricchoice.com) Using the upper end of 8 cents/kWh with solar panels, it will take about 231,213 kWh for the solar panels to pay for themselves. Given that about 10,909 kWh is used by the average household per year, at the current rate, it would take 21.19 years for solar panels to pay for themselves. However, prices with solar panels are going down, while prices of grid electricity are going up, so the solar panel price will likely pay off sooner.
